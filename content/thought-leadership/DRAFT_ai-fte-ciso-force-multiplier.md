## Post

Every CISO I know is running a 2-person team against a 5-person workload. Most accept it. I stopped accepting it.

Here's the reframe that changed how I think about this:

You don't have a staffing problem. You have an infrastructure problem.

Specifically: 15 years of accumulated systems. No complete inventory. No centralized risk register. Documentation that lives in someone's head — not in a runbook.

That undocumented debt is what actually makes your team feel understaffed. Every incident takes 3x longer without a runbook. Every compliance gap found in an audit costs 10x what proactive closure would have. Every unknown system is an unknown risk.

The traditional answer: hire more people.

The honest answer: headcount isn't coming.

So I built something different.

I configured an AI command center that functions as four specific FTE equivalents:

→ **Security Documentation Agent** — generates SSPs, policies, runbooks, and compliance artifacts on demand

→ **GRC & Compliance Agent** — verifies controls against NIST/FedRAMP/FISMA, flags deviations, prepares audit evidence

→ **Risk Intelligence Agent** — maintains the risk register, surfaces posture changes, packages findings for leadership

→ **Asset Registry Agent** — catalogs systems, vendors, and SLA contracts; finds what you don't know you're responsible for

My 2-person team now has persistent, structured memory: an asset registry, compliance verification workflows, and board-ready briefing templates — available in seconds, not weeks.

The auditors still ask hard questions. The incidents still happen.

But now we walk into the room prepared.

If you're a CISO trying to do more with less: stop waiting for headcount. Start building the infrastructure that lets AI do the work of the FTEs you were never funded to hire.

The leverage is real. You just have to design for it.
